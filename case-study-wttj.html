The product

Brief overview of the product you worked with. What it does, who uses it, why it matters.

The problem

Frame the user need clearly. Who is affected, how often, and what's the real cost to them? Don't just describe the missing feature — describe the experience of the gap.

Research & insights

How did you validate that this was a real problem? Conversations, Reddit posts, app store reviews, personal experience. Show the evidence.

Constraints

What were you designing within? Existing platform patterns, design system, technical feasibility, user habits. This shows you understand design doesn't happen in a vacuum.

Exploration

What directions did you consider before landing on your solution? Include 2–3 alternatives and why you ruled them out. This is the most important signal of UX maturity.

Design solution

Walk through your final design. Explain specific decisions — why you chose this pattern over another, what you were optimizing for. Annotate your screens.

Reflection

What would you do differently with more time? What would you want to test? What are you still uncertain about? Be honest — this shows self-awareness and that you'd be easy to give feedback to.
